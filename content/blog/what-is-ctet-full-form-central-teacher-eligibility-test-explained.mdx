## What is CTET Full Form?

CTET stands for **Central Teacher Eligibility Test**. It is a national-level eligibility examination conducted by the Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) to certify candidates as qualified to teach in Central Government schools and other schools that consider CTET scores.

The exam was introduced in 2011 following the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009 (RTE Act), which made it mandatory for teachers in elementary schools (classes 1 to 8) to clear a Teacher Eligibility Test (TET).

For Hindi-speaking aspirants searching for the **ctet full form in Hindi**, it is "केंद्रीय शिक्षक पात्रता परीक्षा" — directly translated as Central Teacher Eligibility Test. The exam is bilingual, with question papers available in both English and Hindi (and regional languages for some papers), so candidates can attempt it in either medium.

## Who Conducts CTET and Why It Matters

CTET is conducted by **CBSE (Central Board of Secondary Education)**, usually twice a year — typically in July and December, though schedules can shift. The exam is the gateway to teaching positions in:

- Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan (KVS) schools
- Navodaya Vidyalaya Samiti (NVS) schools
- Central Tibetan Schools Administration
- Schools under the administrative control of UTs like Chandigarh, Ladakh, Andaman and Nicobar Islands, Lakshadweep, and Daman and Diu
- Private schools that voluntarily adopt CTET as a benchmark

CTET matters because, under the RTE Act, no person can be appointed as a teacher in elementary education (classes 1 to 8) in a school covered by the Act without qualifying a TET. For Central Government schools, CTET is the only accepted TET; state government schools accept their own State TET.

The syllabus is heavily based on [NCERT full form and why it matters](/blog/what-is-ncert-full-form-and-why-it-matters-for-indian-students), since NCERT textbooks define the curriculum framework for classes 1 to 8 that CTET questions are drawn from.

## CTET Eligibility Criteria for 2026

The **ctet exam eligibility 2026** rules are set by CBSE and follow the National Council for Teacher Education (NCTE) guidelines. Eligibility differs for Paper 1 and Paper 2.

### Eligibility for CTET Paper 1 (Classes 1 to 5)

You can apply for Paper 1 if you meet any one of the following:

- Senior Secondary (or equivalent) with at least 50 percent marks and a 2-year Diploma in Elementary Education (D.El.Ed.)
- Senior Secondary with at least 50 percent marks and a 4-year Bachelor of Elementary Education (B.El.Ed.)
- Senior Secondary with at least 50 percent marks and a 2-year Diploma in Education (Special Education)
- Graduation with at least 50 percent marks and a B.Ed. degree (subject to NCTE notification — such candidates must undergo a 6-month bridge course in elementary education)

### Eligibility for CTET Paper 2 (Classes 6 to 8)

You can apply for Paper 2 if you meet any one of the following:

- Graduation with at least 50 percent marks and a 1-year Bachelor in Education (B.Ed.)
- Graduation with at least 45 percent marks and a 1-year B.Ed. (as per NCTE norms)
- Senior Secondary with at least 50 percent marks and a 4-year B.A.Ed. or B.Sc.Ed. or equivalent
- Senior Secondary with at least 50 percent marks and a 4-year B.El.Ed. or B.A.Ed. or B.Sc.Ed.
- Graduation with at least 50 percent marks and a B.Ed.-Special Education (with 6-month bridge course)

A relaxation of 5 percent in the qualifying marks is available to SC, ST, OBC, and PwD candidates, in line with NCTE norms. Final-year candidates of any of the above teacher training programmes can also apply provisionally, and their CTET result will be considered valid only if they produce the qualifying certificate at the time of document verification.

## CTET Exam Pattern: Paper 1 vs Paper 2 Explained

Understanding **ctet paper 1 vs paper 2** is the most important step before filling the form, because each paper targets a different teaching level.

### Paper 1 — For classes 1 to 5 (Primary Teacher)

Paper 1 has 150 multiple-choice questions, each carrying 1 mark, with no negative marking. The exam is conducted in CBT (Computer Based Test) mode and lasts 150 minutes.

| Section | Number of Questions | Marks |
|---|---|---|
| Child Development and Pedagogy | 30 | 30 |
| Language I (compulsory) | 30 | 30 |
| Language II (compulsory) | 30 | 30 |
| Mathematics | 30 | 30 |
| Environmental Studies (EVS) | 30 | 30 |
| **Total** | **150** | **150** |

### Paper 2 — For classes 6 to 8 (Upper Primary / TGT)

Paper 2 also has 150 MCQs of 1 mark each, with no negative marking, in CBT mode over 150 minutes. The first three sections are compulsory; the fourth section depends on the subject you choose.

| Section | Number of Questions | Marks |
|---|---|---|
| Child Development and Pedagogy | 30 | 30 |
| Language I (compulsory) | 30 | 30 |
| Language II (compulsory) | 30 | 30 |
| Mathematics and Science (for Maths/Science teacher) OR Social Studies/Social Science (for Social Studies teacher) | 60 | 60 |
| **Total** | **150** | **150** |

### Who needs Paper 1 vs Paper 2 — a simple rule

- Want to teach classes 1 to 5 (PRT / Primary Teacher) — appear for **Paper 1**.
- Want to teach classes 6 to 8 (TGT / Trained Graduate Teacher) — appear for **Paper 2**.
- Want to teach both levels — appear for **both papers in the same session** (CBSE allows this).

The difficulty of Paper 1 is calibrated to the NCERT syllabus up to class 5, while Paper 2 is calibrated to the NCERT syllabus up to class 8. Child Development and Pedagogy is common to both, but the focus shifts from early childhood pedagogy in Paper 1 to adolescent pedagogy in Paper 2.

## CTET Passing Marks and Certificate Validity

### CTET passing marks

The **ctet passing marks** are fixed by CBSE based on NCTE norms. The exam is scored out of 150 marks.

| Category | Passing Marks (out of 150) | Passing Percentage |
|---|---|---|
| General | 90 | 60 percent |
| SC / ST / OBC / PwD | 82 | 55 percent |

A candidate who scores the required marks is issued a CTET Eligibility Certificate and a marksheet through DigiLocker. The certificate is digitally signed and legally verifiable, so you do not need a physical copy for most recruitment processes.

### CTET certificate validity

The **ctet certificate validity** is now **lifetime**. Earlier, the certificate was valid for 7 years from the date of declaration of the result. In 2021, CBSE amended the CTET guidelines to make the qualifying certificate valid for life — meaning once you pass CTET, you can apply for teaching posts under its purview at any time without re-qualifying.

There is no restriction on the number of attempts either. A person who has already qualified CTET can also reappear to improve their score, which many candidates do to strengthen their KVS or NVS recruitment profile, since final selection in those organisations is based on a combination of CTET qualification, written test, and interview.

## CTET vs State TET: What's the Difference?

India has two parallel TET systems — CTET (Central) and State TETs (conducted by individual states like UPTET, RTET, MHTET, KTET, TNTET, and so on). Here is how they differ:

| Parameter | CTET | State TET |
|---|---|---|
| Conducting body | CBSE | State education boards or commissions |
| Applicable schools | KVS, NVS, Central Tibetan Schools, UT schools | State government schools, aided schools |
| Language of question paper | English and Hindi (plus some regional languages) | State language and English |
| Validity | Lifetime | Lifetime (in most states after the 2021 amendment) |
| Recognition outside the state | Accepted across India for Central schools | Limited to the issuing state |
| Frequency | Usually twice a year | Varies by state |

If you want to teach in your home state's government schools, you will need the relevant State TET — for example, candidates in Uttar Pradesh may also need to clear [UPSSSC PET full form and eligibility](/blog/what-is-upsssc-pet-full-form-exam-eligibility-pattern) for certain subordinate education posts, alongside UPTET. CTET, on the other hand, is your entry ticket to Central schools and many private chains that recognise it.

## How to Apply for CTET Exam Online

CBSE opens CTET applications through its official portal — ctet.nic.in. The application process for the 2026 sessions typically follows these steps:

1. Visit ctet.nic.in and click on the application link for the relevant CTET session.
2. Register with your name, date of birth, email ID, and mobile number to generate a registration number and password.
3. Fill in personal, academic, and exam centre details in the application form.
4. Choose Paper 1, Paper 2, or both, and select your language and subject combinations.
5. Upload scanned photograph, signature, and relevant category certificate (if applicable), within the specified file size and format.
6. Pay the application fee online through net banking, UPI, or credit/debit card.
7. Download and print the confirmation page for your records.

### CTET application fee for 2026

| Category | Paper 1 or Paper 2 | Both Paper 1 and Paper 2 |
|---|---|---|
| General / OBC | ₹1,000 | ₹1,200 |
| SC / ST / PwD | ₹500 | ₹600 |

The admit card is released around 10 to 15 days before the exam date, and results are typically declared within 4 to 6 weeks of the exam. Answer keys are published first, with a window for challenges before the final key and result are released.

## Common Teaching Exam Abbreviations Related to CTET

While navigating CTET notifications, KVS recruitment, and NVS advertisements, you will encounter several related abbreviations. Knowing them upfront saves time:

- **TET** — Teacher Eligibility Test (umbrella term; CTET and State TETs are subsets)
- **CBSE** — Central Board of Secondary Education (conducts CTET)
- **NCTE** — National Council for Teacher Education (sets eligibility norms)
- **KVS** — Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan (Central school chain that recruits via CTET)
- **NVS** — Navodaya Vidyalaya Samiti (residential Central schools for rural talent)
- **PRT** — Primary Teacher (classes 1 to 5; requires CTET Paper 1)
- **TGT** — Trained Graduate Teacher (classes 6 to 8; requires CTET Paper 2)
- **PGT** — Post Graduate Teacher (classes 9 to 12; CTET does not cover PGT, but some Central schools use separate exams)
- **D.El.Ed.** — Diploma in Elementary Education (2-year teacher training diploma)
- **B.El.Ed.** — Bachelor of Elementary Education (4-year integrated course)
- **B.Ed.** — Bachelor of Education (1-year or 2-year teacher training degree)
- **EVS** — Environmental Studies (a section in CTET Paper 1)
- **CDP** — Child Development and Pedagogy (common section in both papers)
- **RTE** — Right to Education Act, 2009 (the law that made TET mandatory)

## Frequently asked questions

### What is CTET full form in Hindi?

### Is CTET valid for private schools?

Yes, many private schools across India prefer or require CTET-qualified candidates, though it is mandatory only for Central Government schools like KVS and NVS.

### What is the validity period of CTET certificate?

The CTET qualifying certificate is valid for lifetime, as per the CBSE 2021 amendment that removed the earlier 7-year limit.

### What are the passing marks for CTET?

General category candidates need 90 out of 150 marks (60%) to qualify, while SC, ST, OBC, and PwD candidates need approximately 82 out of 150 (55%).

### Can I appear for both CTET Paper 1 and Paper 2?

Yes, if you meet the eligibility criteria for both, you can appear for Paper 1 (for classes 1–5) and Paper 2 (for classes 6–8) in the same exam session.
