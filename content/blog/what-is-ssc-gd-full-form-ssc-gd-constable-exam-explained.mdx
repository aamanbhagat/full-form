## What is SSC GD Full Form?

SSC GD stands for **Staff Selection Commission General Duty**. Breaking it down:

- **SSC** — Staff Selection Commission, the central government body under the Department of Personnel and Training that recruits non-gazetted staff for various ministries and forces.
- **GD** — General Duty, the post category the exam recruits for.

So the ssc gd constable full form is **Staff Selection Commission General Duty Constable**, and the ssc gd exam full form is the same — Staff Selection Commission General Duty Examination. In Hindi, candidates often search for the ssc gd full form in hindi, which reads as **कर्मचारी चयन आयोग सामान्य ड्यूटी**.

The staff selection commission gd full form matters because it tells you exactly who is conducting the exam and what rank you are applying for. SSC is not the force itself — it is the recruiting agency. Once selected, you are inducted into one of the Central Armed Police Forces (CAPFs) or related organisations, not into SSC.

## What Does General Duty (GD) Mean in SSC?

General Duty is a rank-and-file post. A GD Constable is the frontline operational personnel of a paramilitary or central armed force — the person doing border patrolling, internal security duty, law and order support, and camp guarding. "General Duty" distinguishes this post from specialised roles such as tradesmen, clerks, drivers, or technical staff.

The forces that recruit through SSC GD are:

| Force | Abbreviation | Primary Role |
|---|---|---|
| Border Security Force | BSF | Indo-Pak and Indo-Bangladesh border guarding |
| Central Industrial Security Force | CISF | Critical infrastructure and metro security |
| Central Reserve Police Force | CRPF | Internal security and counter-insurgency |
| Indo-Tibetan Border Police | ITBP | Indo-China border guarding |
| Sashastra Seema Bal | SSB | Indo-Nepal and Indo-Bhutan border guarding |
| Secretariat Security Force | SSF | Government secretariat security |
| Assam Rifles | AR | Counter-insurgency in the Northeast |
| Narcotics Control Bureau | NCB | Drug law enforcement |

The ssc gd meaning, therefore, is not just a label — it defines the operational backbone of India's central forces. A GD Constable in BSF will spend most of their career on the border; one in CRPF will be deployed across states for internal security; one in CISF will guard airports, metros, and industrial units.

## SSC GD Constable Exam Overview for FY 2026-27

The Staff Selection Commission conducts the GD Constable exam annually. For the FY 2026-27 recruitment cycle, the process follows the standard three-stage structure:

1. **Computer Based Examination (CBE)** — the written test, held online across multiple centres in India.
2. **Physical Efficiency Test (PET) and Physical Standard Test (PST)** — height, chest, and running requirements.
3. **Medical Examination** — detailed medical fitness check at a CAPF hospital.

The notification for the FY 2026-27 cycle carries the post count, important dates, and force-wise vacancy breakup. Recruitment numbers in recent cycles have ranged between 35,000 and 50,000 posts, making this one of the largest central government recruitment drives in India. The exact vacancy figure for FY 2026-27 is published in the official SSC notification on ssc.gov.in, and aspirants are advised to track the commission's calendar rather than rely on unofficial sources.

The exam is open to both male and female candidates, with force-wise and category-wise reservation as per central government norms. Female candidates are generally considered for CISF, CRPF, and SSB, while BSF, ITBP, and Assam Rifles posts are largely male-only — the exact gender-force matrix is specified in each notification.

## Eligibility Criteria for SSC GD Constable

Eligibility for the SSC GD exam is straightforward but strict. The three core requirements are nationality, age, and education.

### Nationality

You must be a citizen of India. Subjects of Nepal and Bhutan, and Tibetan refugees settled in India before 1962, are also eligible under specific government notifications.

### Age Limit

The standard age bracket is 18 to 23 years as of the cutoff date mentioned in the notification. Category-wise relaxations apply:

| Category | Upper Age Relaxation |
|---|---|
| SC / ST | 5 years |
| OBC | 3 years |
| Ex-Servicemen | 3 years (after deducting military service) |
| Children and dependents of Kargil conflict casualties | 5 years |

### Educational Qualification

The minimum qualification is matriculation (10th class) pass from a recognised board. This is where many aspirants connect their preparation with school-level study material — the [NCERT full form for Indian students](/blog/what-is-ncert-full-form-and-why-it-matters-for-indian-students) is relevant because NCERT textbooks cover much of the General Knowledge and Elementary Mathematics syllabus tested in the exam.

You must have the passing certificate on or before the cutoff date specified in the notification. A provisional marksheet is not sufficient at the document verification stage.

## Exam Pattern and Syllabus Structure

The SSC GD written exam was restructured a few cycles ago. The current pattern is a single-stage Computer Based Examination.

### Computer Based Examination (CBE)

| Section | Questions | Marks | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| General Intelligence and Reasoning | 20 | 40 | 60 minutes (total) |
| General Knowledge and General Awareness | 20 | 40 | — |
| Elementary Mathematics | 20 | 40 | — |
| English / Hindi (language) | 20 | 40 | — |
| **Total** | **80** | **160** | **60 minutes** |

Each question carries 2 marks. There is negative marking of 0.50 marks for every wrong answer, so blind guessing is penalised. The test is bilingual — questions appear in both English and Hindi except for the language section, where you choose either English or Hindi.

### Syllabus Highlights

- **General Intelligence and Reasoning** — analogies, classification, series, coding-decoding, spatial visualisation, non-verbal reasoning.
- **General Knowledge and General Awareness** — Indian history, geography, polity, current affairs, general science, and economic basics.
- **Elementary Mathematics** — number system, percentages, ratio and proportion, averages, profit and loss, time and distance, simple and compound interest, mensuration.
- **English / Hindi** — comprehension, grammar, vocabulary, sentence structure, error spotting.

### Physical Efficiency Test (PET)

Candidates who clear the CBE cutoff are called for PET/PST. The running standards differ by gender and force:

- **Male candidates** — 5 km run in 24 minutes 30 seconds.
- **Female candidates** — 1.6 km run in 8 minutes 30 seconds (standards vary slightly by force).

PST checks height and chest measurements. For male candidates the minimum height is generally 170 cm (relaxed for certain categories and regions), with a chest requirement of 80 cm unexpanded and 5 cm expansion.

### Medical Examination

The final stage is a detailed medical test — eyesight, hearing, dental, general fitness, and any disqualifying conditions — conducted at a designated CAPF hospital. Candidates who fail the medical test on specific grounds may appeal for a review medical examination within the stipulated window.

## SSC GD Salary and Career Progression

SSC GD Constables are appointed at Pay Level 3 of the 7th Central Pay Commission. The post comes with force-specific allowances that make the take-home pay significantly higher than the basic figure suggests.

### Salary Structure

| Component | Amount (approx.) |
|---|---|
| Basic pay | ₹21,700 |
| Dearness allowance | As per central government rate |
| House rent allowance | Varies by posting city |
| Transport allowance | As per norms |
| Ration money | Provided in kind or cash |
| Special duty allowance | Applicable for hard-area postings |

The gross monthly salary typically ranges between ₹35,000 and ₹40,000 depending on posting location and force. Border and high-altitude postings carry additional allowances that can push the in-hand amount higher, and these are a major reason the post remains attractive despite the demanding work conditions.

### Career Progression

A GD Constable can rise through the departmental hierarchy:

1. Constable (GD)
2. Head Constable
3. Assistant Sub-Inspector (ASI)
4. Sub-Inspector (SI)
5. Inspector
6. Deputy Commandant and above (through departmental exams)

Promotions depend on service length, departmental exam performance, and vacancy availability. Many officers reach the Sub-Inspector or Inspector rank through internal departmental exams without appearing for fresh external recruitment, which makes SSC GD a viable long-term career entry point rather than just a constable-level job.

## Frequently asked questions

### What is SSC GD full form?

SSC GD stands for Staff Selection Commission General Duty, referring to the national-level exam for recruiting constables in central armed police forces.

### Is SSC GD held every year?

Yes, the Staff Selection Commission conducts the GD Constable exam annually, with notifications typically released for the FY 2026-27 recruitment cycle.

### What is the salary of an SSC GD Constable?

The basic pay for an SSC GD Constable is approximately ₹21,700 per month, with additional allowances bringing the gross salary higher.
