The full form of **IFSC** is **Indian Financial System Code**. It is an 11-character code that uniquely identifies a specific bank branch in India's electronic payment systems, and you need it for almost every account-to-account transfer.

## Breaking down the 11 characters

- **First 4 letters** — the bank code (e.g. SBIN for State Bank of India).
- **5th character** — always 0, reserved for future use.
- **Last 6 characters** — the branch code.

## Where is the IFSC used?

The IFSC routes money in **NEFT, RTGS and IMPS** transfers. When you add a beneficiary, the IFSC tells the system exactly which branch should receive the funds.

## Where to find your IFSC

You can find it on your chequebook, your bank passbook, the bank's website, or through an IFSC lookup tool by selecting your bank, state, city and branch.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is IFSC the same as MICR?
No. IFSC is an 11-character alphanumeric code for electronic transfers, while MICR is a 9-digit code printed on cheques for clearing.

### Does every branch have a different IFSC?
Yes. Each bank branch has its own unique IFSC code.

### Can I transfer money without an IFSC?
For NEFT, RTGS and IMPS by account number you need the IFSC. UPI transfers use a VPA instead and do not require it.
