## What is UPSSSC PET Full Form?

UPSSSC PET stands for **Uttar Pradesh Subordinate Services Selection Commission Preliminary Eligibility Test**.

Let us expand each part so the acronym becomes easy to remember and recall in an interview or form-filling situation:

- **UPSSSC** — Uttar Pradesh Subordinate Services Selection Commission. This is the state-level recruitment body that selects candidates for Group C and some Group B posts under the UP government. It functions from Lucknow and operates under the UP Subordinate Services Selection Commission Act.
- **PET** — Preliminary Eligibility Test. This is a screening examination introduced to shortlist candidates for various mains-level recruitment exams conducted by UPSSSC.

So when someone asks what is UPSSSC PET full form, the complete expansion is: Uttar Pradesh Subordinate Services Selection Commission Preliminary Eligibility Test.

### UPSSSC PET full form in Hindi

For Hindi-medium aspirants, the same full form is written as:

**उत्तर प्रदेश अधीनस्थ सेवा चयन आयोग प्रारंभिक पात्रता परीक्षा**

- उत्तर प्रदेश = Uttar Pradesh
- अधीनस्थ सेवा चयन आयोग = Subordinate Services Selection Commission
- प्रारंभिक पात्रता परीक्षा = Preliminary Eligibility Test

Knowing the Hindi full form matters because the exam is bilingual, and many official notifications are released in Hindi first. If you are filling the form from a Hindi interface or reading a district-level advertisement, this is the exact phrasing you will encounter.

## What Does the PET Exam Mean for UP Government Jobs?

Before PET was introduced, every UPSSSC recruitment — whether for Lekhpal, Junior Assistant, VDO, or Technician — had its own separate preliminary exam. This created three problems:

1. Candidates had to fill multiple forms and pay multiple fees for different posts.
2. The commission had to conduct separate exams for each post, which delayed the entire recruitment timeline.
3. Aspirants had to prepare for slightly different syllabi for different posts, making preparation inefficient.

PET solved this by becoming a **single common screening test**. Once you qualify PET, your score remains valid for a specified period — typically one to three years depending on the notification — and you can apply for multiple main exams using the same PET score.

Think of PET as an eligibility passbook. You clear it once, and then you become eligible to appear in the main recruitment exams for specific posts that UPSSSC notifies during the validity period.

### Which posts use PET as the screening stage?

Some of the common recruitments that use PET scores include:

- UP Lekhpal (Revenue Accountant)
- Junior Assistant / Clerk
- Village Development Officer (VDO / Gram Vikas Adhikari)
- Tubewell Operator
- Technical Assistant
- Stenographer
- Forest Guard
- Supervisor (Female)
- Conductor (Roadways)

The exact list changes with each notification cycle, so always check the latest UPSSSC advertisement on the official website (upsssc.gov.in) before assuming a particular post is included.

## UPSSSC PET Eligibility Criteria You Need to Know

The eligibility for PET is simpler compared to post-specific main exams. Here is the breakdown that first-time aspirants should memorise.

### UPSSSC PET age limit

| Category | Minimum Age | Maximum Age |
|---|---|---|
| General / EWS | 18 years | 40 years |
| OBC | 18 years | 40 years |
| SC / ST | 18 years | 40 years |
| PwD (General) | 18 years | 55 years |
| PwD (OBC / SC / ST) | 18 years | 55 years |

The age is calculated as on July 1 of the exam year. For the 2026 cycle, candidates born between July 2, 1986 and July 1, 2008 (for the general category) broadly fall within the eligible band, though you should confirm exact cut-off dates from the official notification once it is released.

### UPSSSC PET qualification

The minimum educational qualification for PET is:

- **Class 10 (High School)** pass from a recognized board — UP Board, CBSE, ICSE, or any equivalent recognised by the Government of India.

This is the baseline for the PET exam only. Individual posts may require higher qualifications. For example, Junior Assistant needs Class 12 with typing skills, and Lekhpal may require Class 12. But for the PET itself, Class 10 is sufficient to appear.

### Domicile requirement

PET is specifically for Uttar Pradesh recruitment. While candidates from other states can apply, reservation benefits — OBC, SC, ST category certificates — are applicable only for UP-domiciled candidates. If you belong to another state, you are treated as a general category candidate for reservation purposes, even if you hold a central OBC certificate.

### Other eligibility points

- You must have a valid Aadhaar or another government-issued photo ID.
- Candidates with pending criminal cases must declare them honestly in the application form.
- Only one application is allowed per candidate; duplicate forms lead to automatic rejection.

## UPSSSC PET Exam Pattern and Syllabus Overview

The UPSSSC Preliminary Eligibility Test pattern is designed to test basic awareness and aptitude rather than deep subject knowledge. Here is the structure.

### Exam pattern at a glance

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| Mode | Offline (OMR-based) |
| Number of questions | 100 |
| Total marks | 100 |
| Duration | 2 hours (120 minutes) |
| Language | Bilingual (Hindi and English) |
| Negative marking | Yes — 1/4 mark deducted per wrong answer |
| Type of questions | Multiple choice (objective) |

### Subject-wise marks distribution

| Indian Economy | 5 | 5 |

The exact distribution may see minor changes in the official notification, but this is the broad structure followed in recent cycles.

### How scoring works

- Each correct answer gives 1 mark.
- Each wrong answer deducts 0.25 marks (negative marking).
- Unanswered questions carry no penalty.
- Your final PET score determines your ranking on the merit list, which is then used to shortlist candidates for main exams.

The cut-off varies by category. In recent cycles, general category cut-offs have ranged roughly between 60 to 75 marks out of 100, depending on the difficulty level and the number of candidates who appeared.

## Difference Between PET and Main Exams in UPSSSC

Many first-time aspirants confuse PET with the main exam. Here is the clear distinction.

| Aspect | PET | Main Exam |
|---|---|---|
| Purpose | Screening and eligibility | Final selection for a specific post |
| Conducted for | All Group C posts (common) | One specific post at a time |
| Syllabus | General awareness, basic aptitude | Post-specific subject knowledge |
| Score validity | Usually 1 to 3 years | Only for that recruitment cycle |
| Number of times you appear | Once per cycle | Once per post you apply for |
| Negative marking | Yes (1/4 mark) | Varies by post |

In simple terms: PET is the filter, and the main exam is the final test. You must clear PET to sit for the main exam, but clearing PET does not guarantee a job. You still need to score well in the main exam for the specific post you want.

### Example flow for a candidate

1. You appear for PET in 2026 and score 80 marks.
2. UPSSSC notifies Junior Assistant recruitment in FY 2026-27.
3. You apply for the Junior Assistant main exam using your PET score.
4. You clear the main exam and the typing test.
5. You get selected for the post based on combined merit.

This is why PET is called a Preliminary Eligibility Test — it is the first step, not the last.

## How to Prepare for UPSSSC PET Effectively

Because PET tests breadth rather than depth, your preparation strategy should focus on covering all subjects at a basic level rather than going deep into any one area. Here is a practical approach.

### Start with NCERT foundations

The General Science, History, Geography, and Economy portions are largely based on Class 6 to 10 NCERT content. If you are starting fresh, reading [NCERT full form and why it matters](/blog/what-is-ncert-full-form-and-why-it-matters-for-indian-students) will help you understand why these textbooks form the backbone of most state-level exams in India.

### Build a weekly study plan

A realistic 12-week plan for a first-time aspirant:

- **Weeks 1 to 4:** History, Geography, and Constitution — one subject per week, plus weekend revision
- **Weeks 5 to 8:** Economy, Science, and UP Special — UP-specific history, geography, polity, and schemes
- **Weeks 9 to 10:** Hindi, English, Mathematics, and Reasoning — daily practice sets
- **Weeks 11 to 12:** Current affairs of the last 8 to 10 months, plus full mock tests under timed conditions

### Use previous year papers

Solve at least the last two PET question papers to understand the difficulty level and question style. This is more useful than randomly buying expensive mock test series. You will notice that the commission tends to repeat question patterns across cycles.

### Current affairs strategy

For current affairs, focus on:

- National and international events from the last 8 to 12 months before the exam
- UP-specific government schemes, policies, and appointments
- Sports, awards, and important days
- Defense news and science developments

Daily newspaper reading — Hindi or English — plus a monthly current affairs compilation is sufficient. Do not overload with five different sources.

### Practice with negative marking in mind

Because PET has negative marking, blind guessing is dangerous. During mock tests, practice leaving questions you are unsure about. A candidate who attempts 80 questions with 70 correct and 10 wrong scores 67.5 marks, while someone who attempts all 100 with 60 correct and 40 wrong scores only 50 marks. Accuracy matters more than attempt volume.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid in PET Application

Every year, thousands of forms get rejected due to avoidable errors. Here are the most common ones and how to steer clear.

### 1. Entering wrong personal details

Name, father's name, and date of birth must match your Class 10 marksheet exactly. Even a missing middle name or a spelling variation can cause rejection during document verification at later stages.

### 2. Uploading incorrect photo or signature

UPSSSC specifies photo dimensions, file size, and background requirements. A blurred photo, a signature on coloured paper instead of white, or a photo with cap or goggles can lead to rejection. Always check the official specification sheet before uploading.

### 3. Selecting wrong category

If you select OBC, SC, or ST but your certificate is not in the UP government format, you will be treated as general category. Central government OBC certificates are not accepted for UPSSSC reservation benefits. Make sure your caste certificate is issued by a UP competent authority and is current.

### 4. Not checking eligibility for the main exam

Some candidates clear PET but later realise they do not meet the qualification for the specific post they want. For example, PET needs only Class 10, but Junior Assistant needs Class 12 plus typing proficiency. Check post-wise eligibility before assuming PET clearance is enough for everything.

### 5. Paying the fee but not submitting the form

The application process has multiple stages — registration, form filling, fee payment, and final submission. Many candidates pay the fee but forget the final submission step. Always check for the confirmation page and download the submitted form PDF for your records.

### 6. Ignoring the correction window

UPSSSC often opens a correction window after the application period closes. If you made a mistake, use this window rather than hoping it will be overlooked. Check the official website regularly during the application cycle for updates.

### 7. Not saving the registration number

Your PET registration number is your identity for all future UPSSSC recruitments that use PET scores. Save it in multiple places — email, cloud storage, and a physical note. Losing it creates unnecessary stress during main exam applications.

## Frequently asked questions

### What is UPSSSC PET full form in English?

UPSSSC PET stands for Uttar Pradesh Subordinate Services Selection Commission Preliminary Eligibility Test, a screening exam for various Group C government posts in Uttar Pradesh.

### What is the age limit for UPSSSC PET?

The general age limit for UPSSSC PET is 18 to 40 years, with category-wise age relaxations applicable for OBC, SC, ST, and other reserved categories as per UP government rules.

### Is UPSSSC PET conducted every year?

UPSSSC aims to conduct the PET annually, but the schedule depends on commission notifications and administrative readiness in a given year.

### What qualification is needed for UPSSSC PET?

Candidates must have passed Class 10 (High School) or an equivalent examination from a recognized board to be eligible for the UPSSSC PET exam.

### Is UPSSSC PET mandatory for all UP Group C posts?

Yes, UPSSSC PET serves as a common preliminary screening test; candidates must qualify it to appear in main exams for most Group C posts notified through UPSSSC.
